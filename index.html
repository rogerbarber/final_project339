What is Coffee?

Coffee is the seed of a coffee plant and taken from its fruit; the most common varieties are Arabica and Robusta.

Coffea arabica

Arabica is considered the high-end and delicious variety that grows in cool, sub-tropical climates and often at higher elevations. It requires tender care over a long period of time. This is the type you want when you buy the "good stuff". Almost everything above generic store-brand coffee is entirely, or mostly, Arabica.

Coffea robusta

Robusta, or "canephora", is the hardier relative of the Arabica plant that grows in a much wider variety of climates. It is more bitter, produces more fruit, and has more caffeine per seed than its sub-tropical cousin. This is often used as a "mixer" to make a coffee blend cheaper and more powerful. It is popular in some countries around the world and enjoyed alone.

What is 'Specialty Coffee'?

Think back to the "good old days" of black coffee. It was all dark, all pre-ground, and questionably sourced. Specialty coffee is known as the "third wave", being the third age of coffee production and consumption. The focus has shifted from providing a cheap, plentiful, and consistent product to providing coffee that is ethically sourced, lighter roasted, well-grown and of a higher standard. It is more about embracing the methods and coffee culture of the regions the coffee is grown. You can learn more about coffee production and coffee preparation at the links within this sentence.
